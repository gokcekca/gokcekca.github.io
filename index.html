Montie House

548 M.A.C. Ave · East Lansing, MI 48823 · (666) 666-6666 · montiehouse@gmail.com

Montie House is an cooperative house located at 548 M.A.C. in East Lansing, Michigan. Built in 1939 as a sorority house, Montie House was founded by Howland House in 1966 as a student-only men's co-op. It was named after Melvin Montie, a charter member of Howland House, who was killed in action during the Korean War. The house is now owned by an independent corporation, comprised of the co-ed group of students who live there.

Meet The Monties

usually held september

This party is supposed to be Monties only, but that breaks down about midnight. It is a great opportunity to meet the new people who have just moved in and get to know them in a relaxed atmosphere. In the nineties, the house rented a hot tub and put it outside the back door in the parking lot.

The Halloween Party

Halloween

There are a lot of parties you could go to around Halloween, but you don't want to miss the Montie Halloween party. An annual favorite, the party requires attendees to dress up as anything, Halloween style. You'll be sure to see some ridiculous costumes.

The Wine & Cheese

November

This semi-formal event traditionally has live jazz music, and is about half the size of the other parties. An intimate soiree, over twenty-five different kinds of cheese are served, and a bottle of wine is required from attendees at the door.

Montie Gras

March

Only the classiest of New Orleans fashion is worn to Montie Gras. For this party, 1920s-style dress is en vogue, and masks are highly encouraged.

What is The Montie?
The Montie House is located near the campus of Michigan State University in beautiful East Lansing. It is a student-owned and student-run cooperative. The residents who have a Constitution to abide by, and describes the house's parliamentary-style procedures. Each member is expected to spend a certain amount of time doing their house job, which is decided by the house manager.

What kinds of amenities do you have?
Full occupancy is forty-two people, but we currently have almost 30 residents. There are twenty-three rooms, several common areas, a huge kitchen, a pop machine, 25-cent laundry, a 37" TV, 5 bathrooms, and some really rad peole.

What if I'm interested in living there?
First, call (517) 332-8641, and ask for a tour of the house. After you decide to live in Montie, a $400 deposit is required. To share a double costs $285, but if you want to live by yourself, single rooms cost $335. Residents can also choose to pay $50 to have home-cooked meals Sunday through Thursday.
